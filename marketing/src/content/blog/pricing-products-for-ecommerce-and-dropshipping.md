Most online sellers lose money not on bad products, but on bad pricing. They set a price that "feels right," forget half their costs, and wonder why the bank balance never grows.

## Start with your true landing cost

Your landing cost is everything it takes to get one unit ready to sell:

- Product cost (converted to your selling currency at today's rate)
- Shipping and customs
- Packaging
- Advertising cost per unit

Add these up. This is the floor — sell below it and you lose money on every order.

## Then add fees and margin correctly

Marketplace fees and payment fees are a **percentage of the selling price**, not the cost. That's the trap: if you just add a fixed markup, fees eat your margin.

The correct formula divides your landing cost by `(1 − fees% − margin%)`:

```
Selling price = Landing cost ÷ (1 − marketplace% − payment% − target margin%)
```

This guarantees your target profit *after* fees, every time.
