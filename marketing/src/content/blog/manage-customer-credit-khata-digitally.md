In most Pakistani businesses, credit — *udhaar* — is normal. Customers buy now and pay later, and you keep a running tally in a register. The problem isn't credit; it's **untracked** credit.

## Why the paper khata fails

- Entries get missed during a busy rush.
- You can't quickly see who is overdue.
- There's no reminder system, so balances drift for months.
- If the notebook is lost or damaged, so is the money.

## A better system

A digital customer ledger ties every sale and payment to a customer automatically. At any moment you can see:

- Total outstanding across all customers
- Exactly who owes what, and for how long (aging)
- A one-tap WhatsApp reminder with the balance and a payment link

## Get paid faster

The businesses that collect best do two simple things: they **review aging weekly** and they **send polite, automatic reminders**. Both take seconds when your khata is digital.
